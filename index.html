ADKAR through COM-B

ADKAR describes a sequence of individual states: Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement. It's useful for sequencing a change plan, but on its own it doesn't say why a person is stuck at a given stage. Michie's COM-B system supplies that diagnostic layer: every stage of ADKAR draws on some combination of Capability, Opportunity, and Motivation, and naming which one is missing changes what intervention actually fixes it.

Below, the six COM-B sub-components are mapped against each ADKAR stage, then typical change deliverables are placed against both, so the discovery, strategy, and implementation work has a clear line back to what it's actually meant to move.

01 — The overlay
Where each ADKAR stage sits in COM-B

What "primary" and "secondary" mean here. The COM-B paper describes capability, opportunity, and motivation as an interacting system rather than a checklist to score separately. Michie, van Stralen, and West (2011) draw arrows both into and out of each component: opportunity can act on motivation, capability can act on motivation, and performing the behaviour itself can then reshape all three. They also note that the barrier to one behaviour might be capability alone, to another it might be opportunity alone, and to a third it might require changes across all three.

A primary driver below is the component that stage's behaviour cannot occur without, the condition the paper treats as necessary. A secondary driver is a component that acts on the primary one through those causal arrows, a contributing condition rather than the direct target. Practically, this means fixing a secondary driver on its own rarely closes the gap, since the necessary condition is still unmet.

The gap ADKAR hides: an "Ability" problem and an "Opportunity" problem look identical from the outside — someone isn't doing the new behaviour yet — but they need opposite fixes. If the barrier is Opportunity (no time, conflicting systems, an unchanged process), more training won't move it; you have to change the environment. ADKAR's Ability stage bundles both together, which is where change plans built on ADKAR alone tend to over-invest in training and under-invest in removing structural barriers.
